# 01 ¬∑ Principle: Why ln(E) and ln(T)?

## Origin of the method

The significance test implemented in this repo comes from the landmark paper:

> Wu, Z. & Huang, N. E. (2004). *A study of the characteristics of white noise
> using the empirical mode decomposition method*. **Proc. R. Soc. Lond. A**,
> 460(2046), 1597‚Äď1611.

This paper gave EMD a firm statistical footing. Selecting "meaningful IMFs"
no longer has to rely on eyeballing or subjective experience ‚ÄĒ we now have a
rigorous **hypothesis test** to fall back on.

## Core idea: null-hypothesis testing

We take the null hypothesis $H_0$: "the input is pure white noise." Under
$H_0$, each IMF of the decomposition has a specific theoretical location on
the $\ln(T)$‚Äď$\ln(E)$ plane. If a real-signal IMF lands far from that
location, we reject $H_0$ and conclude the IMF carries genuine physical
content.

## Four mathematical properties

### 1. Dyadic filter-bank behaviour

Wu & Huang demonstrated through extensive numerical experiments that
**EMD acts like an adaptive dyadic filter bank on Gaussian white noise**.
Higher-order IMFs have roughly twice the mean period of the previous one:

$$T_m \approx 2 \, T_{m-1}.$$

### 2. Energy‚Äďperiod conservation

White noise has uniform spectral energy density. Wu & Huang showed that for
pure white noise, the $m$-th IMF's energy density $E_m$ and mean period
$T_m$ satisfy

$$E_m \cdot T_m = \text{const}.$$

### 3. Log-log gives a line with slope ‚ąí1

Taking the natural log of both sides:

$$\ln(E_m) + \ln(T_m) = c \quad\Longrightarrow\quad \ln(E_m) = -\ln(T_m) + c.$$

**On the $\ln(T)$‚Äď$\ln(E)$ plane, white-noise IMFs fall on a straight line
with slope $-1$.** This is the geometric heart of the test.

### 4. Chi-squared ‚Üí confidence bounds

IMF amplitudes are approximately Gaussian, so their energy (sum of squares)
follows a **chi-squared distribution**. Wu & Huang (2004) equations
(2.13)‚Äď(2.17) give: for the $m$-th IMF the degrees of freedom are
$k_m = N / T_m$, and the $100(1-\alpha)\%$ confidence interval is

$$\ln(E_m) \in \big[\, -\ln T_m + c + \ln\tfrac{\chi^2_{\alpha/2, k_m}}{k_m},\;
-\ln T_m + c + \ln\tfrac{\chi^2_{1-\alpha/2, k_m}}{k_m} \,\big].$$

`emdsig.confidence.chi2_confidence_bounds` implements this directly.

## Further reading

- **Flandrin et al. (2004, 2005)** ‚ÄĒ extends the filter-bank analysis to
  **fractional Gaussian noise (fGn)**; the slope is no longer $-1$ but a
  function of the Hurst exponent $H$. `emdsig.colored_noise.red_noise_slope`
  provides the corresponding correction.
- **Huang, Shen & Long (1999)** ‚ÄĒ the original EMD paper; recommended prior
  reading.
- **Wu & Huang (2009)** ‚ÄĒ the EEMD paper that addresses mode mixing.
- **Torres et al. (2011)** ‚ÄĒ CEEMDAN, the decomposer this repo recommends
  by default.
